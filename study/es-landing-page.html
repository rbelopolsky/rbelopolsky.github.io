I. Goals

My goals were: to update the look and feel of the brand. I wanted it to express that this was a simple, secure, reliable and trustworthy product that would be easy to use and rewarding for their users. I also wanted to improve the registration form to make it as user friendly as possible. Lastly, I wanted the page to look great regardless of which device or browser the user was using.

II. Research

I had already spent years working on and researching trends in landing page design, so I knew a few things I wanted to introduce off the bat. White space was going to be essential to this design. I know there are high converting and messy squeeze pages that work well but my issues with those types of landers are: modern users are becoming more savvy and starting to expect a certain level of design to have trust in a product, as well as the LTV of users accrued this way is generally much lower than users gained from a landing page that has enough content to inform and set expectations.

Also, plenty of research was coming out around this time putting to bed the old golden rule of “above the fold content is king.” Mobile devices and tablets are becoming the primary method of browsing the internet. I had seen the trend prove true with our own user base. So scrolling would be second nature to a vast majority of users.

Expressing trustworthy-ness is the key to any good landing page and just having an awesome design is not enough. I needed to add third party testimonials that users could believe in. Having already worked on a deal between our parent company and Trustpilot, I added this brand to our package and worked with our support team to accrue a few hundred testimonials on their new Trustpilot page. Luckily their users turned out to be quite happy to share and were simply not given the opportunity in the past.

Keeping things simple for the user was the last ingredient I needed. I worked with support team to write a succinct 3 step how it works section.

III. Hypotheses

  1. The new overall design would improve conversion rates
  2. Improvements to the registration form would decrease registration errors
  3. Creating a design that was consistent across devices and browsers would see an overall jump in conversion rate

VI. Results

  1. The new overall design would improve conversion rates.
    The new page showed 33% increase in conversion rates.
  2. Improvements to the registration form would decrease registration errors.
    Password errors decreased by 56%.
  3. Creating a design that was consistent across devices and browsers would see an overall jump in conversion rate.
    Bounce rates for mobile devices dropped 32%. Bounce rates for Chrome users dropped 17%.
